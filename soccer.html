One squad.

We're 23 players deep and we only play together a few months a year. This is a plan to keep the same crew on the field all twelve months — shrinking and stretching the roster to fit whatever's available: 11v11, 8v8, 7v7, futsal, sand, or a Saturday we organize ourselves.

The math

23 players is a superpower

Most teams can't do this — they scrape to field 11. We can field two 7v7 sides at once, absorb vacations, injuries, and work travel, and still never forfeit. Nobody commits to everything. You commit to the seasons and trips that fit your life, and the squad is always big enough to cover the rest. The team stays one team: one name, one kit, one group chat — the format is the only thing that changes.

The operating model

How we run it

Opt in per season

Nobody signs up for the year. Each league, tournament, or trip gets a thumbs-up window in the chat — in or out, no guilt either way.

Two captains per format

Whoever's keenest on a given format runs it — collects fees, sets the roster, done. Spreads the load so no one person burns out.

One kitty, small dues

A modest per-season contribution covers league fees, field rentals, and balls. Tournaments and trips priced separately for those who go.

One identity

Same name, same colors, every format. Over a couple of years that's a reputation — and the team other squads want to schedule.
